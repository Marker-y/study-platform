1. Definition of Evolution

Two definitions:

  1. Darwin: "Descent with modification"
  2. Modern: Change in relative frequency of alleles in a population over time

2. Key Terms

TermDefinition
PopulationGroup of organisms of same species that live in particular area at same time
SpeciesGroup of organisms that have potential to interbreed in nature and produce viable, fertile offspring
Gene PoolAll alleles present in a population
Allele FrequencyNumber of times an allele occurs in gene pool compared to other alleles
Important: Sterile offspring = different species
  • Mule (male donkey + female horse) = sterile → donkey and horse are different species
  • Zedonk (zebra + donkey)
  • Zebroid (zebra + horse)

3. Charles Darwin & the HMS Beagle (1831)

Darwin's Voyage

  • 1831: Set sail on HMS Beagle
  • Collected plant and animal specimens
  • Key observations led to theory of evolution by natural selection

4. Darwin's Observations

A. Adaptations

  • Many plants/animals well-suited to their environments
  • Even in harsh environments, organisms survived and reproduced
  • Adaptation: Inherited characteristics that increase organism's chance for survival

B. Fossils

  • Fossils: Remains or imprints of once-living organisms found in rock layers
  • Some fossils resembled living organisms; others did not
Example: Armadillo (living) vs. Glyptodon (extinct) — similar structures!

C. Galápagos Islands

  • Islands close together but have different climates
  • Characteristics of animals/plants varied among islands

Finches:

  • Only 1 species of finch on South American mainland
  • Darwin identified 13 species on Galápagos
  • Species differed in beak size and shape
  • Beak size/shape associated with diet
Conclusion: Original finches reached islands, dispersed to different environments, changed over generations

6. Lamarck's Theory (and Why It's Wrong)

Lamarck's 3 Ideas:

  1. Tendency Toward Perfection
  2. Use and Disuse (use it or lose it)
  3. Inheritance of Acquired Traits (stretch your neck → pass long neck to offspring)

Why It's Wrong:

  1. Evolution is NOT predetermined; NOT about being "better"
  2. Acquired characteristics are NOT inherited (only genetic traits are inherited)
Memory trick: Lamarck thought if you work out and get big muscles, your kids will be born muscular. Wrong! Only DNA changes are inherited.

Positive Contributions:

  1. Recognized species change
  2. Established link between environment and organism's body structures

7. Darwin's Big Ideas (1859)

A. Descent with Modification

  • Organisms share characteristics → had common ancestor in remote past
  • Over time, organisms accumulated diverse modifications/adaptations
  • Adapted to their specific environments

B. Natural Selection (The Mechanism)

Darwin's 3 Observations:

  1. Traits are often heritable
  2. Offspring vary in their heritable traits
  3. Species produce more offspring than environment can support → many fail to survive and reproduce

Darwin's 2 Inferences:

  1. Individuals with favorable characteristics tend to leave more offspring (fitness = survive AND reproduce)
  2. This unequal ability leads to accumulation of favorable traits in population over generations
Natural Selection Definition: Process where individuals with certain heritable traits survive and produce at higher rate because of those favorable traits.

8. Evidence for Evolution

A. Direct Observation

  • Peppered moths: Industrial melanism (light vs. dark moths)
  • Soapberry bugs: Beak length evolution
  • Drug-resistant bacteria: Evolve quickly due to fast reproduction + mutation

B. Homology

Homologous Structures: Same structure (common ancestry), different function → Evidence for divergent evolution
Example: Turtle, alligator, bird, mammal limb bones — same bone pattern, different uses!
Analogous Structures: Same function, different structure (NO common ancestry) → Evidence for convergent evolution
Example: Bird wings vs. insect wings — both fly, but completely different structure!
Vestigial Structures: Reduced in size, no longer serve important function → Evidence of evolution from ancestors
Examples: Whale pelvic bones, snake pelvic bones/spurs, human tail bone

C. Embryology

  • Early stages (embryos) of many vertebrates are very similar
  • Same groups of embryonic cells develop in same order → produce tissues/organs

D. Conserved Genes/Proteins

  • Important genes are highly conserved (don't change much over time)
  • Example: Cytochrome c (protein in electron transport chain) similar across species

E. Fossil Record

  • Paleontology: Study of fossils
  • Documents pattern of evolution over millions of years
Principle of Superposition: Bottom rock layers are older (contain more primitive creatures)

F. Biogeography

  • Biogeography: Study of geographic distribution of species
  • Similar environments don't always have similar organisms (penguins in Antarctica vs. polar bears in Arctic)
Reason: Evolution can only work with what's present
Continental drift/Pangaea: Explains why organisms on islands resemble those on nearest mainland

9. Mechanisms That Change Allele Frequencies (Causes of Evolution)

  1. Mutation: Adds new alleles to gene pool
  2. Natural Selection: Favorable traits increase in frequency
  3. Migration (Gene Flow): Transfer of genes from one population to another
  4. Genetic Drift: Change in allele frequency due to chance events (small populations)

10. Types of Natural Selection

Natural selection affects distribution of phenotypes in 3 ways:

TypeWhat It FavorsEffect on PopulationExample
StabilizingIntermediate phenotypesReduces variation; favors "average"Human birth weight (not too small, not too big)
DirectionalOne phenotypic extremeShifts population toward one extremePeppered moths (dark favored during industrial revolution)
DisruptiveBoth extremes (NOT intermediate)Creates two distinct phenotypesBeak size in finches (small for seeds, large for nuts)
Memory tricks:
• Stabilizing = "keeps the status quo" (favors average)
• Directional = "moves in one direction" (shifts population)
• Disruptive = "breaks apart" (favors both extremes)
